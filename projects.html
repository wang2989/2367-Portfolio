Fundamentals of Engineering Honors Robot

The Fundamentals of Engineering Honors (FEH) course at Ohio State University involves creating a robot from scratch to navigate an obstacle course. In the 2020 Spring semester, FEH students set out to build a robot set in a diner-scenario. Obstacles required usto navigate the course using line-tracking algorithms and GPS, recognize light colors using a sensor, and work with unfamiliar hardware and software libraries.
Within our group, I managed the development of our robot’s software and subsequent algorithms. Algorithms developed by me included using our robot’s light sensors to recognize whether it was on a black line and to adjust accordingly based on the positioning of the line. This allowed the robot to follow designated paths set by black lines. Additionally, I created a program to manually change distance values and sensor thresholds in our code to supplement faulty hardware that would’ve allowed the same function.This drastically reduced our testing times from 3-4hours to only 1.

OHI/O Hackathon Judging Lead

One of the best parts about being a Computer Science and Engineering major at Ohio State University is its massive community in both the university and the city of Columbus. I joined OHI/O to help expand the tech community at OSU and see the inspirational projects created by students. Our club organizes OSU’s official hackathons, such as HackOHI/O and MakeOHI/O, which provide students an opportunity to demonstrate their skills and passion and connect with industry representatives.
In the organization, I coordinate project judging. This involves asking industry professionals to become judges, creating criteria to judge the teams on, planning the logistics of judging, and handling prizes. This past year, I’ve focused on developing a 2-stage judging process to award our prizes to teams in a more objective fashion. While there have been setbacks due to Covid-19, I’m always looking at how to improve our judging process so that participants feel motivated to compete. This upcoming Spring 2021 semester, I am also leading our annual MakeOHI/O event.

Judging Web Application

Judging is always a hectic and fast-paced process at OHI/O’s hackathons. We must track numerous judges and teams in a short time span and ensure that everything flows smoothly. To do so, the judging team has been working to create a web application that will handle project data and score submissions by judges. The backend was developed using Django and handles participant information, team data, and judge information. I assisted in planning the structure of our models and am currently working on creating the front-end. Development of the pages includes creating a UI design and using JavaScript to allow for judges to submit a form on the web application and add it to a team’s score.
