OHI/O Judging Lead
One of the best parts about being a Computer Science and Engineering major at Ohio State University is its massive community in both the university and the city of Columbus. I joined OHI/O to help expand the tech community at OSU and see the inspirational projects created by students. Our club organizes OSU’s official hackathons, such as HackOHI/O and MakeOHI/O, which provide students an opportunity to demonstrate their skills and passion and connect with industry representatives.
In the organization, I coordinate project judging. This involves asking industry professionals to become judges, creating criteria to judge the teams on, planning the logistics of judging, and handling prizes. This past year, I’ve focused on developing a 2-stage judging process to award our prizes to teams in a more objective fashion. While there have been setbacks due to Covid-19, I’m always looking at how to improve our judging process so that participants feel motivated to compete. This upcoming Spring 2021 semester, I am also leading our annual MakeOHI/O event.
